Candy introduction

(sweet food)

Candy is a kind of candy cake, which refers to a snack with sugar as the main component. If fruit or nut food is coated with sugar, it is called sweet food (such as sugar gourd).In the broad sense of Asian culture, chocolate and chewing gum are also regarded as a kind of candy. In European and American countries, candy refers only to products made with white granulated sugar or maltose. In ancient times, honey was even used to make candy in Europe and America, but it was not suitable for industrial production because of its too many ingredients that were not easy to control.

Candy can be divided into hard candy, hard sandwich candy, milk candy, gel candy, polished candy, gum based candy, inflatable candy and tablet candy. Among them, the hard candy is a kind of hard and crisp candy with white granulated sugar and starch syrup as the main material; the hard sandwich candy is a kind of hard candy with filling in the candy; the butterfat candy is made of white granulated sugar, starch syrup or other sugar, oil and dairy products as the main material, with egg white not less than 1.5%, fat not less than 3.0%, with special butterfat flavor and burnt flavor; Gel candy is a soft candy made of edible gum (or starch), white granulated sugar and starch syrup (or other sugar) as main material; polished candy is a solid and solid candy; gum based candy is a chewing or bubbling candy made of white granulated sugar (or sweetener) and rubber based material; inflatable candy is a candy with fine, uniform bubbles inside the sugar body. Tablet candy is a kind of candy that is granulated, bonded and pressed.

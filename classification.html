Classification

Classification of common sugars

(According to the refining degree, source, shape and color of sugar, common sugars can be roughly divided into the following categories:)

Coarse sugar:

It belongs to unrefined raw sugar with low purity, many impurities, large water content and light yellow color, such as domestic No. 2 sugar and imported Brazil sugar and Cuba sugar.

Brown sugar powder:

The purity is higher than that of brown sugar, and it's easy to weigh and use.

Glucose powder and glucose syrup:

Powdered glucose is obtained from starch by enzymatic hydrolysis or hydrolysis in the presence of acid. After spray drying, powdered glucose is obtained, with a general water content of 8%.

Malt block and maltose syrup:

Barley and wheat are produced by maltase acting on water. The finished product produced in China is generally called caramel.
